## Repository layout

This repository is a monorepo. Root owns coordination and durable project context: `AGENTS.md`, `receipts_please_game_design.md`, and `.agents/skills/`. The Bun/Vite/R3F application lives at `control-desk/`; run package, typecheck, lint, build, and dev-server commands from that directory. Do not add a root `package.json` merely to proxy the app. Future apps belong in sibling directories with explicit ownership rather than being mixed into `control-desk/`.

Keep local Chrome QA evidence in the root `.codex/` directory and do not commit it. Keep `node_modules/` and build output local to their application directory.

## Current scope

Build the internal R3F production tool first. The current milestone includes:

- a local Bun + Vite + React + TypeScript + Tailwind + R3F/Drei environment;
- `/model-lab` as the default route, with an orbitable/pannable/zoomable scene, overview and close cameras, lighting presets, model registry, selection, inspection framing, and performance visibility;
- `/effects-lab` (labeled **System FX**) for replaying deterministic stamp, paper, printer, migration, freeze-card, screen, object, and camera responses together;
- `/animation-lab` as a composition stage, separate from the asset register, for replaying one-model and multi-model interactions such as a stamp contacting paper, a receipt moving through a decision workflow, a guarded card freeze, a travel-evidence comparison, an animated CEO phone dial, and a fraud response across the computer and card control;
- `/scene-lab` as an explorable desk/environment assembly with free OrbitControls, saved camera views, lighting comparisons, and the actual relative placement used by later gameplay;
- `/audio-lab` as a rights-aware audio intake/gallery surface;
- `/os-lab` as a native 1040 × 585 HTML-only Expense OS workbench that mounts the exact production `GameWorkstation` and stores, with lab-only deterministic manual/Ramp fixtures, resizable/maximizable persistent windows, contextual source-app docks, and working app controls for UI development and visual QA outside the R3F camera projection;
- a focusable workstation screen: clicking the computer display moves into the readable Expense OS, mouse and keyboard input stay scoped to it, `Escape` and a visible close control exit, and the same screen hosts the full manual-to-Ramp case workflow;
- a complete game-grade Expense OS workflow with ten manual cases and six Ramp cases, evidence-backed decisions, a progressing queue, timer/scoring, case-scoped notifications and card controls, local session persistence, and the manual-to-Ramp migration;
- polished procedural R3F hero props plus a growing modular desk/office kit. Six assets are only the first batch, not an environment-completeness claim;
- project-local skills for model creation, audio sourcing, and Chrome visual QA;
- `/` as the canonical user-facing vertical slice, with `/game` retained as a compatibility alias: ten manual cases, including the approved VP Sales client dinner and one unambiguous Fire decision, plus six automatically handled Ramp exceptions, physical desk tools, decisions, required actions, timing, scoring, one-click migration, adaptive prototype audio, and the giraffe ending;
- `/control` as the canonical control-desk entry point, with `/model-lab` retained as a compatibility alias. The specialized animation, scene, effects, and audio lab routes remain available from its navigation.

The current review priority is the hero workstation, decision controls, and authored camera/interaction paths. The boxed office only needs to be coherent and extensible at this milestone; do not spend hero-prop time polishing background filler that does not support a remembered moment.

The user explicitly advanced the project beyond the lab-only milestone. Keep `/game` as a thin orchestration layer over the receipt catalog, registered desk assets, effects, audio, migration state, and the existing scene. Pre-Ramp play must use fragmented records plus the physical calculator, stamps, trays, printer, and freeze control; post-Ramp play must demonstrate receipt matching, policy citations, connected travel/vendor/procurement evidence, pre-spend controls, and an exception-only queue. Freeform document physics, organic employee characters, and branching dialogue remain deferred until the vertical slice is stable.

The embedded workstation Expense OS is the lab counterpart to that game: it carries the same ten manual cases and six Ramp cases with evidence-backed decisions, timer/scoring, local session persistence, and the manual-to-Ramp migration. Keep real authentication, live finance-provider integrations, freeform physical document handling, organic employee characters, and branching dialogue deferred.

We are not using Blender for this hackathon phase. Prefer carefully constructed procedural R3F models. Do not create a low-quality generated GLB merely to have a binary file. Exceptional user-supplied authored GLBs may use the installed `gltfjsx --transform --types --shadows` path when they are clearly stronger than a procedural substitute. Preserve the untouched source, keep generated graphs typed, record provenance and clips, and load a decoder-free source at runtime unless Draco is shipped locally. Current exceptions are the giraffe and `merged_animations_3_clips.glb`; both have unknown redistribution rights and must not ship publicly until cleared.

Use sections 10–13 of the design brief as the asset backlog, in this order: finish the hero desk loop (three distinct stamps, freeze control, calculator/tape, printer, trays, computer, phone, lamp, shredder, nameplate), then the employee window and high-value office furniture/set dressing, then reusable documents and comedy props. Asset count alone is not progress; priority-A interaction silhouettes and the five remembered moments in section 23 come first.

## Product direction after the lab

This is for Ramp's Builders Cup. The later game must earn the Ramp reveal through play:

1. Let the player feel fragmented receipt matching, policy lookup, travel checking, vendor review, and inventory reconciliation.
2. Introduce Ramp at the midpoint as a smooth workflow transformation, not an opening advertisement.
3. Keep the desk, cases, and player judgment continuous while matching receipts, surfacing policy, connecting travel/vendor data, and enabling card freeze actions.
4. Use Ramp branding mainly inside the post-migration software and the transition. Product capability and relief lead; the logo confirms what the player already felt.
5. Preserve the comedy and finance-worker point of view. The contrast is broken process versus unified control, not bad people versus a perfect brand.

The approved game midpoint is now a one-click automation payoff. `/` mounts the full shared **Expense OS**, not a separate simplified game imitation, so its app dock, evidence scratchpad, notifications, audit trail, queue, physical stamps, and card controls all use `useWorkstationStore`. The cold open starts from the physical receipt on the desk: clicking that paper (or the matching accessible overlay CTA) begins the shift, after which the player focuses Expense OS to inspect fragmented records in distinct source applications, presses Escape to return to the desk, and chooses a nearby physical stamp. The manual expense inbox must not summarize employee, policy, Slack, travel, inventory, physical-calculator, or transaction answers; it only provides the receipt and launches those separate systems. The redundant Calculator desktop app is removed; the calculator remains a physical desk tool. The People directory remains pinned in the manual Dock so employment dates are always discoverable, Transactions carries approval metadata such as the VP Sales-approved client dinner, and Policy PDF behaves like a paged document viewer. Both `/` and the `/game` compatibility alias must use this same interaction path. The manual OS remains dark, fragmented, and stressful through all ten manual cases, with reviewed receipts retained in the default All view, cortisol starting at 68 percent, and cortisol rising as source apps are reviewed plus larger receipt and system-effect spikes. Decision-ready physical stamps receive the same restrained amber four-corner cue as the opening receipt. After case ten, the overload beat remains inside the persistent OS as a CEO Slack DM and top-right notification rather than a full-screen HUD. It presents one **Install Ramp** action; activating it immediately pins and foregrounds the Ramp installer window, which owns migration progress. The user-facing game has no Manual / Post-Ramp mode toggle; the completed manual queue and its Install Ramp migration are the only path into the Post-Ramp sequence. Activating Ramp exits monitor focus, shakes the camera/world, and automatically advances the existing six migration stages. Ramp then completes the surfaced evidence, control actions, decisions, six-case exception queue, and automatic-decline ending at a readable 0.75 playback multiplier; linked evidence, applied controls, and recorded decisions must visibly advance inside the Ramp window rather than completing between frames. The office still changes from dark/manual lighting to bright/Ramp lighting, cortisol begins its Ramp phase at 22 percent and drains to zero as the exception queue clears, and the supplied `control-desk/public/brand/low-cortisol.jpeg` image briefly fades in and out. The ending CEO dialogue lives in Slack and must remain on screen until the player clicks to continue: Ramp has cleared the backlog, the board eliminates the player's CFO role, and the CFO budget is reallocated to growth. Only then may the existing service-window camera and demand-loaded giraffe reveal the newly funded Chief Growth Officer. Do not mount a second WebGL canvas for this ending. It is a payoff to the established manual workflow, never an opening ad.

The `/scene-lab` workstation prototype retains its explicit **Try Ramp** prompt and player-advanced migration steps so each authored stage can be reviewed independently. Do not apply `/game` autoplay behavior to the lab review surface.

The official white Ramp lockup lives at `control-desk/public/brand/ramp-lockup-white.svg`. It came from Ramp's official press kit; preserve its proportions and metadata, and do not imply partnership or endorsement outside the Builders Cup context.

## Working process

1. Read this entire file, the relevant project skill, and root `receipts_please_game_design.md` before acting. After any context compaction, handoff, or resumed task, explicitly re-read both this file and the full design brief before trusting a summary.
2. Keep the active milestone narrow. Register semantic asset/effect IDs before connecting later gameplay.
3. Parallelize independent assets and research. Use one fresh-context subagent per individual model; the parent agent owns integration and shared scene changes.
4. Put every asset in the lab immediately. Review silhouette, scale, materials, interaction, animation reset, lighting, camera framing, and performance before adding another scene dependency.
5. Keep effects replayable and deterministic. Separate object animation, camera impulse, and screen/DOM timelines.
6. Treat every visible control as a product contract. Exercise all effect buttons, camera and lighting presets, Grid, Perf, reduced motion, navigation, asset selection, and any composition controls in Chrome before handoff.
7. Review every model from front, rear, left, right, top-biased, and low three-quarter views, then repeat the meaningful animation states. Detached text, buried keys, floating parts, and parent/child drift are release blockers even when the default view looks correct.
8. Build solo assets before depending on them in `/animation-lab` or `/scene-lab`; the combined scene is also a scale, contact, lighting, and camera integration test.
9. From `control-desk/`, run `bun run build`, then use the Chrome visual-QA skill for browser evidence. A successful build is not visual approval.
10. Develop and approve Expense OS changes in `/os-lab` at the monitor's exact 1040 × 585 CSS-pixel contract before reviewing the same persistent component through the R3F projection. The standalone pass must exercise distinct app anatomy, real window controls, notification behavior, manual/Ramp parity, and focus-state persistence; the embedded pass then checks corner hit targets and frame-by-frame projection visibility.
11. Update this file when a decision changes. Do not leave abandoned alternatives in new docs.
12. Do not move the camera when the selected asset or animation composition changes. Free-orbit state persists; only an explicit camera preset choice or `Reset view` may animate to a saved camera.
13. Keep the labs distinct: Model Floor is the isolated construction/material/all-angle register with per-model replay controls; Animation Stage owns solo mechanisms plus deliberately authored multi-model sequence scenes; Desk Scene owns the real-scale workstation, seated reach, environment layout, controllable lighting, and workstation-screen focus; System FX owns coupled selected-object, DOM/screen, camera-impulse, and postprocessing response testing. “Model Floor” and “System FX” are not alternate names for the same view.
14. Keep the performance profiler available in every 3D lab, but default it off so profiling does not tax normal interaction. The render-quality selector owns the shared budget: Low favors constrained devices, Default targets smooth review, and Capture restores expensive shadows, DPR, and postprocessing for authored screenshots. Capture is the initial quality; teammates can explicitly select Default or Low for constrained hardware. `/game` uses Capture for its static opening briefing, switches to Default when the shift begins, and restores Capture on restart. A lost WebGL context must fall back to Low quality with Grid and Perf disabled, await the browser's native context restoration, and expose an explicit reload fallback after a timeout or repeated losses.
14. Keep deployment bandwidth demand-driven. On the game cold open, preload only the manual adaptive music loop and the first paper-pickup cue; fetch later audio when first played. Do not globally preload authored GLBs or mount hidden GLB-backed characters. Preserve source assets locally, but defer the giraffe and optional animated-character downloads until their scene is actually requested.

## R3F transform and React safety

- Model at real-world meter scale with the asset root at a documented origin. Apply display zoom only in the registry/stage wrapper.
- Treat every child `position`, `rotation`, and `scale` as local to its parent. Before changing a nested transform, identify the full parent chain and calculate the intended world result.
- Give one component ownership of each animated transform. Do not animate a parent's axis and the child's same axis unless the additive motion is deliberate and tested.
- Use named refs for moving subassemblies such as stamp head, printer tray, cover hinge, button plunger, and paper exit. Animate the smallest stable group.
- Reset every mutated local transform, material value, and visibility flag before replay. React Strict Mode and rapid retriggers must not accumulate timelines or drift.
- Do not create Three geometry, materials, vectors, colors, or GSAP timelines inside `useFrame`. Memoize reusable values and dispose only resources the component owns.
- Do not wrap the R3F `Canvas` lifecycle in React Strict Mode while Fiber 9.6.1 is installed. React 19's development effect replay invokes Fiber's delayed unmount cleanup, which calls `forceContextLoss()` on the reused renderer. Keep model effects idempotent so Strict Mode can be restored after that upstream lifecycle issue is resolved.
- Keep registry scale separate from real dimensions. An asset that looks correctly sized only because its internal root is arbitrarily scaled fails review.
- Stop pointer propagation inside interactive assets and keep hit targets separate from visible meshes when necessary.
- Keep Three objects and timelines out of Zustand. Store serializable IDs and settings; components own refs and imperative animation state.
- Expense OS case state lives in `useWorkstationStore`: active case/app, submitted and closed case IDs, pinned evidence, immutable audit snapshots, per-card state, score/timer state, notifications, the Ramp-unlocked marker, and semantic scene-event IDs remain serializable. Persist only this sanitized game session under a versioned local-storage key. Timer expiry is distinct from completing the Ramp queue; pause gates all workstation input, and the completed state exposes an explicit full-session restart. `useLabStore` continues to own lab mode, focus/camera, lighting, shared effect runs, and migration phase; `LabScene` reconciles that non-persisted phase from the Ramp-unlocked marker after reload. `LabScene` is also the only bridge that maps workstation scene events onto shared effect intents; workstation components never manipulate Three refs.
- `GameShell` must not reset the persisted workstation store merely because the route mounted. Clear receipt history, decisions, audit state, and app state only for an explicit new-shift/full-session restart; retain the ten-entry receipt review history across normal focus changes, route reloads, and manual-to-Ramp migration.
- `/game` phase/orchestration state lives in `useGameStore`; its expense cases, evidence, decisions, scoring, timer, audit trail, card state, and queue live in the shared persisted `useWorkstationStore`. `LabScene` connects the physical freeze control and stamps to that same workstation store and must gate them while paused, while Ramp automation owns the queue, or after a case closes. Ramp automation satisfies the same evidence and required-action validation before recording each decision.
- Treat `src/data/receipts/receipt-generation.json` as the canonical deterministic receipt catalog. Workstation fixtures may curate its player-visible values for a focused prototype, but must not expose catalog `truth`, hidden comparison answers, or generation notes directly in the player UI.
- During the manual phase, retain all ten playable submissions through the complete pre-Ramp queue. Scheduled arrivals use the prototype Slack ping at high volume, a properly anchored top-right OS notification, an unread Slack entry, and the shared `receipt.submitted` paper-drop event; stop the scheduler and any active alert audio during migration and Ramp mode.
- Receipt damage is part of the submission data presentation. Keep a curated projection of the 11 shipped cases' catalog IDs, seeds, template types, rotation, thermal fade, blur, creases, and stains so receipts remain stable across reloads without bundling catalog `truth` or generation notes into the player client.
- Keep the desk scene rearrangeable through one typed code manifest. Asset world placements, hero camera poses, and authored scene-sequence targets belong there instead of being duplicated across JSX. Zustand owns serializable sequence IDs/runs and focus modes; a scene controller translates those events into component-owned camera, object, and DOM timelines.
- A scene asset is not selected by default. Selection outlines, rings, and diagnostic emissive cues belong only to the isolated model inspector unless an authored effect explicitly calls for them.
- Workstation `Html` input must stop propagation at its own boundary without leaving an invisible gate over active controls. Pointer and keyboard input stay scoped while focused; no monitor, light, or status surface may strobe continuously outside a named effect cue.
- Expense OS uses one persistent desktop shell and semantic app registry across manual and Ramp eras. Manual evidence lives in separate draggable, focusable Transactions, Directory, Slack, Policy PDF, Travel, Inventory, Receipt Viewer, and Calculator windows with real z-order, close, minimize, and restore behavior; the inbox never summarizes their answers. Post-Ramp opens the unified Ramp workspace inside that same desktop instead of swapping to a separate interface, while retaining the shell's menu bar, dock, notification placement, window state, window grammar, and authored icon language. Store app icons and supporting UI imagery as centralized assets; placeholder letter tiles and emoji are not acceptable production icons.
- A transformed Drei `Html` screen is not interaction-complete until its DOM hitboxes match the visible monitor pixels. During authored camera motion it may remain transform-mounted for spatial continuity, but the settled focused state must expose a monitor-aligned interactive surface verified with `getBoundingClientRect()`, `elementFromPoint()`, and real Chrome pointer actions at the center and edges.
- Workstation focus uses the authored seated eye point and keeps Expense OS mounted only on the physical monitor; never replace it with a separate floating screen-space panel. Mount one persistent monitor DOM subtree and project its four authored screen corners into a fixed-size CSS surface so camera motion changes only its projective transform, not its layout or React identity. Focus exit/entry must preserve open applications, window positions, z-order, and notification state. Keep the right decision wing visible and raycastable; the physical APPROVE, REJECT, and FRAUD stamps submit `approve`, `reject`, and `investigate` through the same evidence validation, score, audit, and scene-effect path as OS controls.

## Quality gates

- No visibly faceted blockout aesthetic, default-material look, unexplained chunky proportions, floating contact, z-fighting, shadow acne, broken normals, or one-angle-only asset.
- Validate inspection, player, profile, and overview cameras under manual, studio, Ramp, and night lighting.
- Animation needs anticipation, contact/state change, secondary motion, settle, and exact reset. Reduced motion must preserve meaning.
- Shared effect IDs describe intent, not a canned motion. Each model must respond in a way that is unique to its mechanism and physical role; a global camera impulse may supplement but never substitute for model-specific behavior.
- The operator camera must originate from a plausible seated eye point, preserve usable reach/readability, and avoid intersecting the chair or desk. Model-inspection camera labels must describe inspection views rather than reusing gameplay terminology such as `player`.
- Desk Scene exposes a true seated first-person review camera derived from the desk anchor, currently `[0,1.28,1.03]`, with a 54-degree vertical FOV and yaw limited to `±0.40π`. Dragging turns the view left/right within that authored forward-office arc; the shell must remain finished throughout the allowed view so the intentionally open rear is never exposed. It does not orbit the player around a target. Every desk-wide animation must stage its important action inside that allowed arc. Exact free-orbit state is restored when leaving temporary workstation or giraffe focus.
- Desk layout is a real spatial-design pass, not a prop pile. The surface must be large enough for hero controls, maintain clear left/manual and right/decision zones, keep cables attached to believable sockets, and leave the screen and input devices unobstructed.
- The current desk integration contract is a true 2.84 m × 1.52 m concave oval/horseshoe with a local finished surface at y=.780. Its local bounds are x ±1.42 and z −.68…+.84; the rounded operator recess is 580 mm deep with a 1.40 m opening, shoulders at x ±.70/z=.70, and a center edge at z=.26. Grommet centers are x −.88/0/+.88, y=.782, with side z=−.445 and center z=−.515. In the room, add the exported desk anchor `[0,.07,.12]`, giving a world surface height of .850. Keep the center input field at x ±.60/z −.34…+.21, fan manual tools continuously along the left wing, and fan stamps/freeze/trays along the right wing using all useful points of `FINANCE_DESK_CONTRACT.wingPlacementArc`; never regress these props into straight rows or allow their contact footprints to cross the desktop edge. The filing cabinet is detached from the desk and staged as a clickable left-side room decoration. Manual/decision props remain directly clickable and trigger their own authored response. Recompute scene coordinates against this contract when a prop envelope changes.
- `OfficeRoomShell` owns the boxed scene envelope and exports the authoritative room anchors. Its local envelope is x −4.20…+4.20, y 0…3.24, z −4.80…+2.40 with finished floor y=.07 and an intentionally open +Z camera edge. Place the desk at `[0,.07,.12]`, `OfficeServiceWindow` at `[0,0,-4.66]`, and the giraffe root at the forwarded `[.76,0,-6.88]` reveal anchor. Do not duplicate the service module's glazing, corridor, skyline, or giraffe bay inside the shell.
- `DeskComputer` exposes `DESK_COMPUTER_SCREEN`; derive the focus-camera target from that exported local anchor plus the computer’s scene position. Mount the Drei `Html` through the computer's optional screen child slot so it inherits the same screen anchor, animated assembly, and every outer transform; do not render it as a world-space sibling or duplicate an untracked screen coordinate. Workstation focus saves the exact free-orbit camera/FOV/target, disables orbit while focused, and restores that saved view on Escape or the visible close button.
- The narrative nameplate keeps the stable `contractor-nameplate` asset ID for integrations, but its approved visible copy is now **HEAD OF FINANCE**.
- The giraffe is absent in the normal scene and at the start of its animation composition. The ending/review sequence is an authored missing → rise → hold entrance, not a loose orbit suggestion: it saves the current camera, performs a forced zoom to the service window, raises the giraffe from below the occlusion line, and holds there behind a clear click-to-exit affordance. The Desk Scene sidebar can run it directly. Clicking or pressing Escape exits, hides the giraffe again, and restores the exact prior camera.
- Preserve source, author, license, modifications, scale, orientation, and animation metadata for every external or generated asset.
- Use authored GLB only when it already exists and passes the lab. Run it through the installed `gltfjsx --transform --types --shadows` CLI; never hand-transcribe its graph.
